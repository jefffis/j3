Email outreach to prospects / users can be a very successful mode of communication. It can also be a big #fail if not done properly.

Here’s a recent example I ran into – from WePay.com

Eh, Big Deal—the Logo’s the Same

To the laymen, perhaps it’s no big deal. But to design nitpickers like me, it IS a big deal. More importantly, subconsciously it’s a big deal to the laymen. All the subtle design choices from the website are nowhere to be found in the email. Instead; new, poorly chosen elements have been added:

  • the logo is right-aligned (a big email no-no) AND has an odd green border added on the right
  • a thick, heavy-feeling black border outlines the entire active area
  • font colors and links very greatly from the website
  • the headline of the email feels like a great afterthought
  • the heinous use of amateurish drop-shadows on the buttons is disruptive (while disruptive can be good, this isn’t the case here)
  • the subtle color sections from the site disappear, making the whole flow of content non-exisitent

All the design choices made on the website [seemingly] were done with care and consideration of the users and the business objectives. The email looks like it was slapped into a free email template (like all Constant Contact templates....yuck) and sent off. Perhaps the person in charge knew that email has great ROI. However, as with most of the world, not all email is created equal.

I’d be curious see what the metrics of this email were. Even if they were decent, I guarantee a bit of planning and professional design care would help these campaigns reach the next level.
