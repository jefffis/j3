The times they are a-changin’. In many more ways than one, but that’s definitely the understatement of the year. Web technologies change seemingly every few minutes, users expectations of said technologies change as well, some faster than others though. It’s a different world when we go to bed than it was when we awoke.

It’s quite normal for me to feel overwhelmed from time to time. I mean, how can I be relevant to a company or a client when I don’t know what just happened on the web just now? I certainly feel like this is a hard question to answer at times. Other times, however, I feel it’s not such a hard question to answer. Most companies and clients dont’t even come close to using said cutting edge technology or advances, at least not for a decent amount of time that is.

So What’s This About

As things change, so do people. I’m ending my time at my current job soon, and with it my half-in / half-out role. For many years, I’ve been a jack of all trades, with a background in art, and a lifelong interest in design, and recent enjoyment of code. But, with my new positon, comes a new title and a new role: CSS Developer and full fledged nerdom. I’ve always been hesitant to take this sort of plunge, and while not a complete change, say if I were to follow my love of LPs (which I can’t figure out how to monetize), I felt this was a great opportunity to do so.

The company I’m joining is a web-only company, something I’ve wanted to be part of for a long time. They are also a RubyonRails (RoR) shop, which I’m really jazzed about as well, plus I do love working with code; so it was the right time for me. Eventually, I’d love to run my own shop, but until I have more experience with a framework like RoR and just how a web company works, I’d rather tag along for the ride.

What About the Pretty Pictures

Leaving the design behind is probably going to be quite the transition for me—I’ve always wanted, and will always want, to have a [large] hand in how something looks. But for now, I’ll keep myself busy with neat side projects and living vicariously through twitter and dribbble. My goal is to be back into the design realm in a few years, but with a much richer development background as well.

Wish me luck.
